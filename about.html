About Me

About Me

Hello! I’m Catriona Anderson. I have just finished my Electronic Engineering and Creative Computing degree at Queen Mary University, and I’m super excited to dive into some real-world projects and start working with interesting people. This September, I’ll be starting a Master’s in Human-Computer Interaction at UCL to further explore my interest in user experience and research.

I’m a creative person at heart, and I love finding new ways to approach problems and come up with solutions that actually make a difference. I’m also a bit of a people person — I love chatting with others, hearing their perspectives, and figuring out how to make things better for them. At the same time, I’ve got a pretty logical mindset, so I’m good at breaking down problems and working through them step by step.

One of my favorite parts of my degree has been exploring Human-Computer Interaction and learning about theories like distributed cognition and phenomenology. It’s been so interesting to understand how people interact with technology and how little changes can make a big difference in the user experience. I also really love digging into research and figuring out what people actually need, even when they’re not totally sure how to put it into words.

I’m really keen to get involved in some exciting projects where I can bring both my creative and analytical sides together. I love work that involves a mix of people-focused tasks and structured, thoughtful problem-solving — especially when it’s research-based. I know I’ve got a knack for figuring out what users or customers are missing and coming up with practical ways to meet those needs.

I can’t wait to start working with real companies and get stuck into some hands-on, impactful projects. Feel free to reach out if you want to chat!
