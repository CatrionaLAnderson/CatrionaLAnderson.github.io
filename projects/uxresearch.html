Overview

Hybrid university lectures often create an engagement gap between in-person and online students. This UX research project explored how lecturer interactions and technology impact student inclusion in hybrid learning environments.

My Role & Skills Gained

  • Conducted ethnographic research across 8 hybrid lectures.
  • Performed student interviews & observations to analyze engagement barriers.
  • Applied thematic analysis to identify UX challenges.
  • Designed a Hybrid Presence Robot to improve online student participation.

Skills Gained:

  • UX Research & Ethnographic Methods
  • Data Analysis & Thematic Coding
  • UX Design Thinking & Problem Solving
  • Concept Design

Key UX Findings & Solutions

  • Online students experience passive learning & limited engagement.
  • Lecturers unconsciously prioritize in-person students.
  • Hybrid lecture tools like Echo360 lack real-time interaction.

Proposed Solution:

Designed a Hybrid Presence Robot that:

  • Projects online students into the physical lecture space.
  • Uses audio, LED indicators & movement tracking to create presence.
  • Mimics in-person engagement, ensuring equal participation.

Lessons Learned

This project deepened my understanding of UX research methods and how technology & behavior impact user engagement. I learned how to conduct qualitative research, analyze user pain points, and propose UX-driven solutions for real-world challenges.

Tools & Technologies Used

  • UX Research – Ethnographic study, observations, interviews.
  • Data Analysis – Thematic analysis (Braun & Clarke, 2006).
  • UX Design – Solution ideation, user-centered design thinking.
  • Figma – Mockups & concept visualization.
